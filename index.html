Projects

May 2024 - Optimization of Quantum Gates using Genetic Algorithm

In this project, I reproduced the work of previous NMR Lab members at IISER Mohali, exploring the optimization of quantum gates using genetic algorithms. By applying genetic algorithms to three-qubit unitary matrices, they designed time-optimal and robust pulse sequences, achieving high theoretical fidelity. Experimental implementation on a three-coupled NMR qubit system validated the approach's effectiveness. As a testament to my understanding, I independently reproduced all results, and the code is available on GitHub for interested readers (see resources section).

March 2024 - Phase Transitions in the Hubbard Model on a 2D Square Lattice

In this project, as part of my Condensed Matter Physics course, I explored the Hubbard model on a 2D square lattice using Python. The objective was to develop a phase transition diagram depicting the transitions between ferromagnetic, antiferromagnetic, and non-magnetic phases. This was achieved by varying the Hubbard Hamiltonian parameters, including hopping t, onsite Coulombic repulsion U, and electron filling. This project was a rework of the original study conducted by Y. Claveau, B. Arnaud, and S. Di Matteo.
